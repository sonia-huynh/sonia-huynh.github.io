Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a model used to understand Māori health, it is also known as the four cornerstones of Māori health, Taha Tinana - Physical wellbeing, Taha Whānau - Family wellbeing, Taha Hinengaro - Emotional and Mental wellbeing, and Taha Wairua - Spiritual wellbeing. Together, these four dimensions determine a peron's wellbeing and health.

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going out for a stroll after dinner with my friend or my family. Playing badminton with my friends on the weekend.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Having dinner with my family every night and talking about my day and listening to them talk about their day.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Taking breaks out of my day to do something I enjoy. Reading, chatting with my sister and my friends, watching a show.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Dedicating quiet time to myself and also engaging in positive affirmations.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Having a stretch outside and enjoying the sunlight in the garden.
