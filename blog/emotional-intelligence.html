Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

EQ stands for emotional quotient and describes the emotional and social aspect of the person, how they view, control, assess and express emotions. EQ is measured using self-reporting tests on questions about personal or another person’s feelings, thoughts and behaviour.

What is the difference between EQ and IQ

Emotional Quotient Intelligence Quotient
Describes the way an individual identifies, and understands how they and others feel. Describes the intellectual way a person thinks, and their overall cognitive abilities through academic knowledge, memory, logic and problem solving
Measured using self-reporting tests on questions about personal or another person’s feelings, thoughts and behaviour Measured through intelligence tests and then scored against a specific IQ scale.
Often associated with empathy, interpersonal communication, emotional awareness and mental resilience. Used in personal and professional relationships. Often associated with accomplishment and achievement, used in educational and employment settings
Skills include:
  • Clearer self expression
  • Active listening
  • Empathy
  • Stress and pressure management
  • Emotional regulation
  • Self aware
  • Mindfulness
 Skills include:
  • Problem solving
  • Critical thinking
  • Logical reasoning
  • Memory
  • Analysing
  • Learning and understanding
  • Comprehension

Describe why EQ is important

From the description of EQ above, we can see that EQ is important as it helps foster better relationships with others through qualities of empathy, emotion management and relating to others to connect with other people.

Having good EQ will allow people to be better communicators as they can express themselves more clearly while also knowing how to actively listen to others. This is relevant not only in the workplace but also in social settings as it can lead to better understanding of other people’s perspectives resulting in more open-minded conversations and also the ability to deal with social conflicts.

In a personal setting, having good EQ can result in better mental health as individuals will have a better understanding of their own emotions which can lead to better emotional control, more thoughtful decisions and also having strategies when it comes to handling stress and pressure.
