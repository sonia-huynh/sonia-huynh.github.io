Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term goal is to be in a career where I am continuously learning new concepts, solving problems and always thinking of new ways to improve and do things. I currently don’t know exactly what job I want but it will probably be something along the lines of a junior developer.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

My strength when it comes to learning is that I am always eager to learn new concepts and since I don’t have a background in tech, it means that everything that I’m learning is fun and new to me.

Limitations I have is, sometimes focusing too much on the small details and spending a lot of time trying to get things right or to understand it completely before moving on. To counter this, I need to understand that I need to take a break and come back to it later, or to look at the bigger picture instead and move on with my task and go back to it if it prevents me from moving forward in my learning.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Being more confident in asking questions and presenting my own work and in a group setting. I also want to learn to accept that we are all starting the course on different experience levels, and not to feel discouraged if other people understand a concept faster or already know it, but to use it as an opportunity to learn from others.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

  • An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives
  • What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

For the sprints, I create a rough timetable for what I want to complete each day, this gives me a guideline on what I want to achieve each day and also makes sure that I am staying on track and managing my time wisely.

I found that Toggl was a really good way to keep me accountable for how much time I spend on each task and also how much work I am doing each day. I plan to continue using something similar to Toggl to keep track of my work productivity.

As I am using a timetable to see what I want to achieve each day, I hope it will help prevent me from overworking one day and not doing enough the next. I also think it is a good way to make sure I am not leaving tasks till the last minute and then feeling stressed. However, if things do build up and I don’t think I have enough time to complete my tasks, I will make sure to let the Facilitators know before it gets too hard.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

I plan to first try and solve the issue on my own either through trial and error or Google, ChatGPT and Youtube tutorials. If none of that works, I will then check whether the question has been asked previously on the Discord chat, and if all else fails, I’ll ask for help from the Facilitators and my peers.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

I expect the facilitators to let us know what their expectations are and to also provide constructive feedback. I also expect the facilitators to give clear answers to questions or a clear guide on where to look to figure out the answer and not something vague that would lead to more confusion than before the question was asked.

Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It's important to plan these in.

I currently don’t have any commitments that will occur at the same time while I am doing the bootcamp. I’m planning to have my down time during the weekends so that I can hang out with my friends and family, but apart from that I don’t see any scheduling conflicts.
