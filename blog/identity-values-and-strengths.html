Identity, Values and Strengths

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

During uni we had to write a lab report based on the results your lab group obtained during the lab. The results my team and I got were not within the normal expected range demonstrated in class. Due to this, a few of my lab mates wanted to forge different results so that the lab report would be easier to write and not have the results affect our grades.

When my lab mates asked me about changing the lab results I had to pick between my value of goal achievement of getting the best grades possible or valuing honesty and integrity in my work at all times.

For me, this situation really emphasised my personal value of honesty and integrity. If we forged our results, it was likely the lecturer would not pick it up, however, I believed that even though our results were out of range, they were what we had observed during the lab so, it was more important to me that we remained truthful with the results we gathered than to forge new results just to be able to write an easier lab report and get a better grade.

Although this did cause a riff between me and some lab members, till today, I still believe I made the right choice in staying true to my values and not being swayed.


Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My friends and family around me all have similar values of strong work ethic, perseverance and family. By being around them, I believe that I have also adopted those values as a person.

In my group of friends and family, we all have strong work ethic and perseverence where we work hard to achieve our own personal goals, to grow ourselves and do the things that we enjoy doing. This pushed me to quit my previous job and go into a coding bootcamp because I also wanted to work hard to pursue my goal of continuous growth through learning new things.


Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Some of my strengths are hard work, perseverance and curiosity to learn. I find that once I have an interest in something, I will work hard to learn about it and play around.

This was also the case for me when I wanted to learn how to crotchet, I taught myself through watching youtube videos and playing around and following tutorials.

I started off with small projects like keychains and a cute amigurumi toys before crocheting my own cardigan and festive santa hat clips for my family.

Because of these strengths, I believe it will support me in my learning journey as I will want to work hard to learn as much as I can and also persevere through any difficult concepts.


Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Some of my limitations in terms of learning would be trying to learn too much in one go due to wanting instant gratification, this can affect my learning journey as it can lead to quickly burning out or feeling frustrated. So, I just need to remind myself to take a step back and remember it is not a race to learn as much as I can all in one day, but to take it easy as I learn new concepts.


Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

At work there can be times when tasks are split in a team and everyone takes some responsibility to work on tasks together, however sometimes there are people who are less willing to help out as they may have other tasks they are working on. This can lead to some tension as some team members are doing more work than others.

During situations like these, I have talked with the member who believes they have too much work, to understand why they can't help out as much and to see what we can do to try alleviate their workload but also for them to still contribute to the team work. Talking it out with the team members was effective because it allowed for open communication and for me to understand their point of view of the situation instead of me jumping straight to the conclusion that they don't want to help out.

Other strategies I could use would be to find the underlying cause of why one team member has so much work and see whether that issue can be resolved at the root so that it wouldn't lead to overwork and tension within the group.

So, looking back, I think taking a step back and assesing the overall root cause of the problem and not just addressing the small problem would be a useful way of solving the tension.
