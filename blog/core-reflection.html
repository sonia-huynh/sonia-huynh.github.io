Core Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I have learnt that it is important to not just focus on the technical skills when learning and that it is important to also focus and not neglect the mental wellbeing and emotional intelligence side.

Some of the core exercises of reflecting on myself and really delving into my values and exploring my growth mindset and emotional intelligence were quite new and different to how I usually approached learning. I feel like my mindset before was more about learning the concepts and achieving good grades and I didn’t focus much on the soft skills, so it was a nice change to know that DevAcademy doesn’t just want you to do well in technical skills but also to build up your Human skills.

I also learnt that it takes time and effort to keep up my mindfulness and that it is important not just to focus on learning, but also to take a break mentally too.

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

They are there to let us understand that it’s not just about coding but that we are all humans with different values, strengths and skills. Our values are different for everyone and they represent the things that are important to us and what make us who we are. It is important to know each other's values so that we respect and understand the people we are working with. It is important to have empathy and be self-aware and open-minded especially in a career that involves working with a diverse range of people on projects. Together, these soft skills allow for building solid relationships with others.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

Understanding that knowing about a growth mindset and actually thinking with a growth mindset is very different. I think that i need to cut myself some slack in the sense that coding is still all pretty new to me and that even though right now i am stuck on a lot of the challenges, by having a growth mindset i need to realise that as long as i am able to attempt them and use some of the code that I have learnt, i am learning a new skill and not to think with the fixed mindset that i am haven’t learnt anything just because i am stuck.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Keeping consistent with practising my mindfulness activities, and constantly reflecting on myself and my mindset. As these are not activities that I usually practise on a daily, it took a bit of adjusting to get used to doing them and understanding why we do them.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

I think it’s important for us to realise that yes, we are here to learn coding skills, but it is important to know that we will also be working alongside other people and that we should take the time to learn about Human skills too so that we understand more about ourselves, other people, our mindsets, values and wellbeing overall in order to build good relationships with the people we will be working together with.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, i think that it is refreshing that this course teaches about core, back in uni, it was all about just learning and there wasn’t any focus on human skills and learning about your own learning mindset, why you are doing what you’re doing and also what your values and other people’s values are, so I think it’s a good thing to be learning this as it means that we are more aware of ourselves and others as it focuses on helping us build more lasting relationships.
